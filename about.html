About the project

Helping families choose with confidence.

Little Local is a student-built website prototype focused on the practical details parents and caregivers consider before visiting a local place.

The problem

Traditional ratings leave important questions unanswered.

A restaurant may have excellent food and still be difficult for a parent with a stroller. An attraction may be highly rated but too loud for a young child. A business may be affordable but lack clean bathrooms or safe parking.

Little Local brings those details together so families can make choices based on their own needs.

Designed for

  • Parents with young children
  • Caregivers planning local outings
  • Families with stroller or accessibility needs
  • Visitors comparing practical options

The rating model

Seven categories, one practical score

The Little Local score is based on seven areas that directly affect a family visit.

Category What it measures
Bathrooms Cleanliness, changing-table access, and convenience.
Staff Patience How welcoming and understanding employees are with children.
Wait Time Whether a typical wait is reasonable for families.
Stroller Access Doorways, aisles, seating space, and movement through the location.
Noise Comfort Whether the environment feels calm, lively, or overwhelming.
Parking & Safety Parking access, lighting, traffic, and safe entry.
Family Value The overall quality and usefulness of the experience compared with cost.

Project management

More than a website

This project was managed as a complete digital initiative. The repository includes a scope statement, work plan, risk analysis, retrospective, README, and published website.

Defining measurable results and clear exclusions helped prevent scope creep while still leaving room for future development.
